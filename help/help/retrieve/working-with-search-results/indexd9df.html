Working With Search Results
<p>Search results will appear in the bottom of the middle pane with the first result selected (making it visible in the preview pane).</p>
<ul>
<li>The fields can be sorted by clicking on the column headers (<strong>Type</strong>, <strong>Title</strong>, <strong>Created</strong>, <strong>Modified</strong>, or <strong>Keywords</strong>).  The sorted column will have an arrow next to it with an arrow indicating it is sorted in descending (down arrow) or ascending order (up arrow).</li>
<li><em>Single</em> click on any of the first three columns (<strong>Checbox</strong>, <strong>Type</strong>, or <strong>Title</strong>) to view the document in the preview pane on the right.</li>
<li><em>Double</em> click on any of the first three columns to open the document.</li>
</ul>
<div>
<p><img title="search_results_detail" src="http://www.docstar.com/help/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/search_results_detail-570×115.png" alt="" width="570" height="115" /></p>
<h2>List Action</h2>
<p>The <strong>List Action</strong> dropdown menu displays the options that are available for the selected item(s).  You can select more than one item in the results by clicking multiple check boxes.</p>
<img class="size-medium wp-image-111" title="retrieve_list_action" src="http://www.docstar.com/help/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/retrieve_list_action-570×226.png" alt="" width="570" height="226" /> The "List Action" Menu from the search results
<p>The following options are available in the  <strong>List Action</strong> menu. </p>
<p><strong>View</strong></p>
</div>
<p>This opens the document(s) in the <strong>View </strong>pane.  Click here for details on the View pane.</p>
<p><strong>Save a Copy</strong></p>
<p>This will download the selected file(s) in a .zip file called "Documents".</p>
<ul>
<li>Depending on your browser and system settings, it will either download to your default location or allow you to specify where to download the file.</li>
<li>Depending on your browser and system settings, subsequent downloads will be called "Documents(1).zip, Documents(2).zip, etc" or ask you to rename the file.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Merge</strong></p>
<p>This will merge two or more selected documents into <em>one single </em>document,  retaining the name of the first document in the list.    The other documents will be deleted after the merge.</p>
<p><strong>NOTE</strong>:  You must select two or more documents to merge.</p>
<p><strong>Move To</strong></p>
<ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>NOT WORKING</strong></li>
</ul>
</ul>
<p><strong>Modify Security</strong></p>
<p>This opens the security settings for the selected document(s).  Click here for security settings details.</p>
<p><strong>E-mail </strong></p>
<p>This e-mails the selected document(s) to addressees of your choice.  The attached document(s) will be added to a .zip file called "Documents.zip".</p>
<ol>
<li>Enter the subject of your email in the <strong>Subject </strong>field.</li>
<li>Enter the e-mail addresses of your intended recipient(s)in the <strong>To</strong> field.  Separate multiple addresses with a semicolon.</li>
<li>Click the <strong>Send</strong> button.   An <em>E-mail Sent</em> message displays briefly.   The sender of the email appears as <em>Support </em> in your recipient(s) inbox.</li>
<li>Click the <strong>Close </strong>button to close the dialog box.</li>
</ol>
<p>The Sender of this email will appear as "Support" in your recipient(s) inbox.</p>
<p><strong>Print</strong></p>
<p>Printing will only work with one document selected at a time. Click <strong>Print </strong>to open your computer’s Print dialog box.  Refer to your operating system’s guide(s) for printing details.</p>
<p><strong>Remove Folder(s) and/or Inbox(es)</strong></p>
<p><strong></strong>Eliminates an inbox/folder and all subfolders, but documents within them are <em>not</em> deleted.</p>
<p><strong>Remove from Inbox</strong></p>
<p>Takes items out of the inbox, but both the inbox and the items removed still exist.</p>
<p><strong>Delete</strong></p>
<p>Deletes the selected folder or inbox as well as any contained documents.  Contained documents are no longer searchable.</p>
